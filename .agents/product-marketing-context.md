## Origin Story (founder POV — use this voice)

> I've always been a sucker for power rankings. For years I spent every Tuesday night building them manually for my fantasy league. Then I finally automated the whole thing. Here's exactly how I did it:
>
> **1. I stopped pulling stats manually.** Every major fantasy platform has an API — ESPN, Yahoo, Sleeper. The data is all there, you just have to know where to find it. Once you tap in, your entire league's stats are available in seconds.
>
> **2. I stopped calculating rankings by hand.** Win-loss record is lazy. Real power rankings factor in recent form, scoring trends, schedule strength, and which teams are peaking right now. Build the right algorithm once and it does the work every single week.
>
> **3. I stopped wrestling with Canva.** The data means nothing if nobody looks at it. Graphics that are clean, branded, and ready to drop in a group chat are what actually start conversations. Presentation is half the battle.
>
> **4. I stopped being the only one who cared on Tuesday nights.** When the rankings hit the group chat the league woke up. Debates. Trash talk. Someone always had a problem with where they ranked. The data wasn't just information — **it was chaos fuel.**
>
> **5. I turned all of it into one tool.** ESPN. Yahoo. Sleeper. Football, baseball, basketball, hockey. Power rankings, win probability, draft grades, full league history — all automated, all shareable.
>
> I built it because I needed it. I think you do too.

This is the canonical UFD founder narrative. Other marketing skills should pull voice cues, problem framing ("Tuesday night," "wrestling with Canva," "chaos fuel"), and the founder-as-user angle from here.

## Product Overview

- **One-liner:** Keep your league talking. All season.
- **What it does:** UFD is the all-in-one analytics dashboard for your fantasy league. Automated power rankings, live win probability, draft grades, and full league history — pulled straight from your Sleeper, ESPN, or Yahoo league, with one-tap shareable graphics built for the group chat.
- **Product category:** Fantasy sports analytics dashboard (the all-in-one)
- **Product type:** B2C SaaS, web app
- **Business model:** Freemium with 7-day free trial (no credit card)
  - **Free** — $0, limited feature access
  - **Individual** — $7.99/mo or $49/yr ($4.08/mo). Covers all your leagues, all sports, just you. *"Best for: the data guy in the league."*
  - **League Pass** — $29 one-time, one league, 365 days, every member auto-included. *"Best for: commissioners who want everyone in."*

## Problems & Pain Points

- **Core problem:** The native ESPN, Yahoo, and Sleeper apps give you raw box scores and standings — that's it. There's no real analytics, no live win probability, no shareable graphics, no league history. So fantasy leagues default to vibes-based trash talk and forget half their own history.
- **Why alternatives fall short:**
  - **Native apps (ESPN/Yahoo/Sleeper):** barebones, single-platform only, no cross-sport, no analytics layer, ugly screenshots
  - **FantasyPros / Dynasty Genius / smaller sites:** usually football-only (or one-sport), don't connect to *your specific league*, no shareable graphics layer, no all-time history
  - **Reddit / Twitter takes:** generic, not about *your* league
  - **Manual spreadsheets / Canva:** the founder lived this for years — Tuesday nights gone, nobody else updates them, ugly when you do
- **What it costs them:** Boring group chats. Weak takes. Losing arguments because nobody has receipts. Half the league checks out by Week 8.
- **Emotional tension:** Wanting to be "the data guy" / the smart one. FOMO mid-week over not knowing where they stand. Frustration that their league's history is invisible. Tuesday night dread of building things by hand.

## Competitive Landscape

- **Direct:** ESPN Fantasy app, Yahoo Fantasy app, Sleeper app — *fall short because* they only show their own platform, give you bare minimums (record, score, basic standings), no analytics, no shareable graphics, and no cross-platform / cross-sport view.
- **Secondary:** FantasyPros, Dynasty Genius, smaller fantasy analytics sites — *fall short because* they're typically football-only (or single-sport), don't connect to your actual league, don't do live Monte Carlo win probability, and aren't built around the group-chat / shareable graphics layer.
- **Indirect:** Manual spreadsheets, screenshots from the platform app, Canva, "vibes" trash talk in the chat — *fall short because* they're work, ugly, and never settle anything.

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**
- **All three platforms** (Sleeper + ESPN + Yahoo) in one place — no other tool covers all three
- **All four sports** (football, baseball, basketball, hockey) — almost every competitor is football-only
- **Both formats** — points and H2H category leagues handled correctly per format
- **Real Monte Carlo win probability** — 10,000 simulations updated daily. *"Not a guess. Not vibes. A number."*
- **Shareable branded graphics** — built specifically for the group chat. The unique wedge.
- **All-time league history** — H2H matrix, Tale of the Tape, season-by-season records, awards
- **Customizable power rankings formula** — adjust the weights yourself
- **League Pass model** — single $29 buy covers the whole league, no codes, no signup hassle for members
- **Built by a fantasy player who actually used it for years** — not a generic SaaS product. The founder spent Tuesday nights doing this manually.

**Why customers choose us:** It's the only tool that turns *your* league's actual data into stuff you can drop in the chat without doing any work — across whatever sport, platform, or format you happen to be in.
